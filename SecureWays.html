SMART TRAFFIC SURVEILLANCE

BACKGROUND
•In the modern era, where urbanization and technological advancements are reshaping our cities, ensuring traffic safety has become a paramount concern. With the rise of vehicular traffic and the complexity of urban roadways, traditional methods of traffic monitoring and enforcement are proving insufficient. However, the integration of intelligent systems, particularly Closed-Circuit Television (CCTV) surveillance, offers a promising solution to enhance traffic safety.

•CCTV surveillance systems have long been utilized for security purposes, but their potential in traffic management and safety is increasingly being recognized. Through the deployment of advanced algorithms and analytics, CCTV cameras can be transformed into intelligent sensors capable of detecting various traffic violations, identifying hazardous situations, and facilitating swift responses to mitigate risks.

SEGMENTATION OF ACCIDENTS


#Segmenting accidents based on their intensity allows for a more efficient allocation of resources and response efforts. Here’s a proposed approach, ranging from low to high intensity:

1.Low-Intensity Accidents (Closed Ones): Low-intensity accidents include minor collisions, fender-benders, or non-injury accidents that do not pose immediate threats to public safety.

2.High-Intensity Accidents(Hospitals and Emergencies):  High-intensity accidents include severe collisions, multiple casualties, or incidents involving hazardous materials that pose significant risks to public safety.

3.Catastrophic Accidents (Hospitals/Emergencies/Closed Ones):  Catastrophic accidents involve mass casualties, major infrastructure damage, or large-scale disasters that overwhelm local emergency response capabilities.
